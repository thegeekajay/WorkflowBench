Why teams need this

Demo success is not launch confidence.

WorkflowBench is built for teams shipping AI into business processes where edge cases, escalation paths, and policy compliance matter. It makes evaluation feel like running tests instead of running a one-off demo.

01

Anecdotal demos hide failure modes

It is easy to make a workflow look impressive in a happy-path demo. It is much harder to prove it behaves correctly across real cases.

02

Launch decisions need a common scorecard

Engineering managers and solution architects need the same benchmark output to judge readiness, compare versions, and explain tradeoffs.

03

Reliability should be reusable

Open-source workflow cases, deterministic scorers, and local-first reports make it easier for smaller teams to validate quality before rollout.

  • Capex approval routing

  • Safe escalation paths

  • Regional access rules
  • Manager approval required

Open-source project positioning

A reliability signal for teams building real AI systems.

WorkflowBench is not trying to be a full eval platform. It is a lightweight benchmark harness that helps smaller teams prove quality before rollout and compare changes over time.

1
Shows engineering depth, not just prompting

It tells a stronger story about systems thinking, launch quality, and operational judgment than a simple chatbot demo.

2
Makes launch risk discussable

Benchmark reports expose failure clusters, comparison runs, and workflow-level regressions in a manager-friendly format.

3
Useful even without a hosted platform

Teams can define cases locally, run a suite on their own adapters, and still produce reports that are easy to share.
